[english] How do I monitor the peak load of my task queue within a ThreadPoolExecutor subclass?
[english] This method wraps the standard submission process to gather metrics. It leverages `self._work_queue.qsize()` immediately after submission to track how busy the system is at that moment.
<switch:python>
[python] def submit(self, fn, *args, **kwargs):
        """Submits a callable to be executed with the given arguments.

        Count maximum reached work queue size in ThreadPoolExecutor.max_queue_size_reached.
        """
        future = super().submit(fn, *args, **kwargs)
        work_queue_size = self._work_queue.qsize()
        if work_queue_size > self.max_queue_size_reached:
            self.max_queue_size_reached = work_queue_size
        return future
<switch:english>
[english] By comparing the current `work_queue_size` to `self.max_queue_size_reached`, you ensure that the tracker always holds the highest load encountered since initialization.
[english] Does this tracking happen correctly if tasks are submitted in rapid succession, potentially leading to concurrent checks?
[english] Since the check happens synchronously within the submission method before the work is picked up by a worker, it accurately reflects the size at the moment of queuing for that specific call.
[english] This implementation makes tracking queue spikes much easier.
